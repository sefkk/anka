Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how ANKA Association ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" (remain on your device until deleted or expired) or "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser).

2. What Data We Collect

When you visit our website and interact with our cookie consent banner, we collect the following information:

  • IP Address: Your Internet Protocol (IP) address, which helps us identify your network location.
  • Location (Country): Your country location based on your IP address.
  • Login Timestamp: The date and time when you accept or reject cookies.
  • Logout Timestamp: The date and time when you leave our website.
  • Device Brand: The manufacturer of your device (e.g., Apple, Android, Microsoft, Linux).
  • Device Type: The type of device you are using (e.g., web/desktop, phone, tablet).
  • Internet Connection Type: Your network connection type (e.g., WiFi, cellular, unknown).

This information is collected automatically when you interact with our cookie consent banner and is stored securely in our database.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to store your consent preferences and to collect the technical information listed above. This helps us:

  • Remember your cookie consent choices
  • Understand how visitors use our website
  • Improve our website's security and functionality
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements

We do not control these third-party cookies. Please refer to the respective privacy policies of these third parties for more information.

6. Managing Cookies

6.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner with two options:

  • Accept: By clicking "Accept", you consent to our collection and storage of the data described in Section 2.
  • Reject: By clicking "Reject", you decline our use of cookies. However, we will still collect and store your data (including IP address, location, device information, and timestamps) as described in Section 2, but marked as "rejected" in our system.

Your choice will be stored in a cookie for one year, so you won't be asked again during that period.

6.2 Browser Settings

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may impact your experience on our website, and some features may not function properly.

6.3 How to Manage Cookies in Popular Browsers

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site permissions

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals.
